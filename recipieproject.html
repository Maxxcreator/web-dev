Gulab Jamun

Ingridients:

  • DRY MILK
  • MAIDA
  • GHEE
  • SUGAR
  • WATER

Process:

To make gulab jamun, mix khoya (milk solids) and a bit of flour into a dough, then shape it into small balls. Fry these balls in ghee until golden brown. Prepare a sugar syrup flavored with cardamom, rose water, and saffron. Soak the fried balls in the warm syrup for a few hours until they absorb the syrup and become soft and juicy. Serve warm or at room temperature.

Kazu Katli

Ingridients:

  • CASHEWNUTS
  • SUGAR
  • GHEE
  • ROSEWATER
  • WATER

Process:

To make kaju katli, start by grinding cashews into a fine powder. In a pan, combine the cashew powder with sugar and a little water to make a smooth paste. Cook this mixture over low heat, stirring continuously until it thickens into a dough-like consistency. Add a bit of ghee and cardamom powder for flavor. Remove from heat and knead the dough slightly. Roll it out between two sheets of parchment paper to about 1/4 inch thickness. Cut into diamond shapes and let cool. Optionally, garnish with edible silver foil (varak) for an elegant touch. Enjoy your delicious kaju katli!

RASGULLA

Ingridients:

  • CURDMILK
  • ROSEWATER
  • SUGAR

Process:

To make rasgullas, start by boiling milk and curdling it with lemon juice to make chenna (fresh paneer). Drain and rinse the chenna, then knead it into a smooth dough. Form small, smooth balls from the dough. In a large pot, prepare a sugar syrup with water and sugar, bringing it to a boil. Gently drop the chenna balls into the boiling syrup, cover, and cook for about 15-20 minutes until they expand and become spongy. Let the rasgullas cool in the syrup before serving. Enjoy these soft, syrupy delights chilled or at room temperature.
